# Project Brief

Last updated: 2026-07-19

## Working title

Comic Creator

## Stage

Product definition. Everything below is a working hypothesis until the user
finishes describing the intended product.

## Current product hypothesis

Children aged roughly 9–13 often have stories they want to tell but cannot turn
them into a finished visual artifact because drawing a complete comic is a high
barrier.

Comic Creator should let a child author the story, plot, characters, and
dialogue while directing AI illustration. The intended result is a comic the
child recognizes as their own work, with the option to export or print it as a
book.

## Intended audience

- Primary: children aged approximately 9–13 who enjoy inventing stories.
- Supporting: parents, guardians, or educators who help children create safely.

## Core promise

Screen time that produces a story the child made instead of content the child
only consumed.

## Authorship principle

The child should remain the author and director. AI should remove the drawing
barrier without silently taking over the plot, dialogue, or creative choices.
The exact interaction model is not yet decided.

## Success hypothesis

A first-time child can move from an idea to a short, coherent, visually
consistent comic and can explain which creative choices were theirs.

## Open product questions

- What is the smallest complete comic-making journey?
- Is the primary experience web, tablet, desktop, or another surface?
- How are characters kept visually consistent across panels?
- How much story assistance is offered without replacing the child as author?
- How are child safety, consent, privacy, and data retention handled?
- What output formats are required for screen viewing and printing?
- Is the best hackathon category `Apps for Your Life` or `Education`?
- What is deliberately outside the Build Week MVP?
